My Journey with AnnieCannons My name is Latysha, and I'm a student in AnnieCannons' Coding Training Program. Before I found AnnieCannons, I never imagined that learning to code could change the entire direction of my life. As a survivor of human trafficking, I came into this program looking for more than just a job skill — I was looking for a way to rebuild. AnnieCannons was founded in 2014 by Jessica Hubley and Laura Hackney with a simple but powerful idea: economic independence is one of the most important tools survivors have to break the cycle of exploitation. That idea is the foundation of everything I've experienced here.

The program is trauma-informed, which means it's built around the reality that many of us are healing while we learn. I've spent months in the classroom — about 400 hours total — working alongside Technical Instructors, Engineering Managers, and a Student Success Coordinator who checks in on more than just my grades. I've learned HTML, CSS,moving from Digital Basics all the way through Web Development.

What makes this program different from a typical coding bootcamp is that we don't just learn on hypothetical projects — we build software that solves problems we've actually lived through. My classmates have worked on tools like TRO Express, which helps people navigate the process of filing a restraining order, and other projects addressing the underreporting of sexual assault. Every project reminds me that the skills I'm gaining aren't just for me — they're for my community too.

AnnieCannons has shown me that financial independence isn't just about a paycheck. It's about safety, stability, and the ability to say no to being re-exploited. Since the program started, 70% of AnnieCannons graduates have gone on to secure employment, and students have collectively earned millions of dollars building real software for real clients. I'm still in the middle of my journey, but for the first time, I feel like I'm building a future instead of just surviving one.
